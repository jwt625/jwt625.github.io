# Fiber fuse effect micro-cavity fabrication

what people do to fibers to get fancy structures always exceeds my expectation. here is how you can get a bubble as a Fabry-Perot cavity *inside* a fiber with these four "simple" steps: 1. introduce periodic hollow damage centers in one fiber using catastrophic self-propelled self-focusing fuse effect 2. cleave it near the damage center, and splice with another good fiber, which blows up the damage into a drop shape cavity 3. cleave the spliced fiber again under microscope so that you could control where the cleaving is. The location affects the final cavity shape 4. splice it again with another good fiber and it will form a more symmetric looking cavity... the micro cavity size can be increased by shocking it again with the splicing arc..
![20251106_035441_0.jpg](/assets/images/2025/20251029_20251110/20251106_035441_0.jpg)
   - transmission sucks but hey look how fun it is D√≠az2018: [A cost-effective edge-filter based FBG interrogator using catastrophic fuse effect micro-cavity interferometers]( https://doi.org/10.1016/j.measurement.2018.03.067)
   ![20251106_035653_0.jpg](/assets/images/2025/20251029_20251110/20251106_035653_0.jpg)
   - this is really glass blowing but inside a fiber. you only need ~ 3 W to form these catastrophic fiber fuse effect periodic hollow voids, and they moves towards the optical signal source at ~ 0.5 m/s. Antunes2014: [Optical Fiber Microcavity Strain Sensors Produced by the Catastrophic Fuse Effect]( https://doi.org/10.1109/LPT.2013.2288930)
   ![20251106_040217_0.jpg](/assets/images/2025/20251029_20251110/20251106_040217_0.jpg)
   ![20251106_040217_1.jpg](/assets/images/2025/20251029_20251110/20251106_040217_1.jpg)
   - the effect itself is as old as from 1987, with very detailed description of the procedure. they tested it in as long as 1.5 km of SMF. Kashyap1988: [Observation of catastrophic self-propelled self-focusing in optical fibres]( https://doi.org/10.1049/el:19880032)
   ![20251106_040633_0.jpg](/assets/images/2025/20251029_20251110/20251106_040633_0.jpg)
