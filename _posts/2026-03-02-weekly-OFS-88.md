# Cryogenic millimeter-wave silicon photonic crystal PCB fabrication

this was a lot of fun, and we started because cryogenic mmw PCBs were too expensive so we had a stupid thought of "why not make one out of silicon wafers that we know how to work with", of course it did not go well. we ran out of resist during the deep silicon etch (DSE), the SEMs were so epic I pulled out my phone on the spot. This is typical from unoptimized DSE and people call them micrograss/silicon grass/black silicon.
![20260223_212150_0.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260223_212150_0.jpg)
![20260223_212150_1.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260223_212150_1.jpg)
![20260223_212150_2.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260223_212150_2.jpg)
![20260223_212150_3.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260223_212150_3.jpg)
   - about a month later we figured out the recipe and etched it thru, while the masked surface was still nice and shiny
   ![20260224_042156_0.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260224_042156_0.jpg)
   ![20260224_042156_1.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260224_042156_1.jpg)
   - however the process was not very reliable, we have tried thicker and thicker resist (SPR220-3 to SPR220-7), spin coat, and pre/post bake recipe, and finally settled with extra step of oxide mask with PECVD and patterning the oxide mask first. Here you see we were still running out of the mask in some region. (and yes picture in the OP was before cleaning away the mask) We also ran out of 3in wafers (they are thinner) at the time so we were running process dev with 4in wafer, and etching thru the wafer in the DSE tool would be bad since there is He gas on the back side, so we actually stacked two 4in wafer together
   ![20260224_042646_0.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260224_042646_0.jpg)
   ![20260224_042646_1.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260224_042646_1.jpg)
   - this was the first near 100% successful batch, felt nice to be able to hold a mmw photonic crystal by hand. recipe still not polished and some regions were not fully etched thru.
   ![20260224_043122_0.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260224_043122_0.jpg)
   ![20260224_043122_1.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260224_043122_1.jpg)
   - half a year after we first started, the yield was pretty much 100%, and we have also realized we can cut the device area by about half. The two regions on the two sides are 2D photonic crystal mirror cells for mechanically holding the central 1D photonic crystal resonator, and we only need one of them to be anle to hold it.
   ![20260224_043510_0.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260224_043510_0.jpg)
   ![20260224_043510_1.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260224_043510_1.jpg)

# Integrated CMOS photonic lasers

if we have more people yearning for really good lasers, these chips that have most components on an optical table shrinked onto them could cost less than 100 bucks.
![20260304_052030_0.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260304_052030_0.jpg)
   - they are not that complicated to make, you have less than 10 different types of components, each with less than 20 parameters that matters. 
   - Heim2026: [Versatile CMOS modulation-free self-isolating stabilizedprecision lasers on a chip]( https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23160) 
   - however it's all analog, and there are a lot of other expensive stuff when you want to use a very good laser for..
   ![20260304_052533_0.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260304_052533_0.jpg)
   ![20260304_052533_1.jpg](/assets/images/2026/20250301_20260304/20260304_052533_1.jpg)
   - bandwidth is more on the gain spectrum of the SOA. if you are going after high power and narrow linewidth, fiber laser with large mode area fiber is the best.
